Quantum Scoring Process

The Quantum Score (also known as Unified Score) is a comprehensive metric evaluating quantum gravity theories across multiple dimensions. It is calculated as a weighted sum of component scores, with multipliers for bonuses, penalties, and category adjustments. The final score is capped at 1.0.

Component Scores

Component Weight Calculation Method Details
Constraints 0.20 Weighted average of passed validators (1=passed, 0=failed) Sub-weights: Conservation 0.40, Metric Properties 0.30, Lagrangian 0.30
Observational 0.25 Weighted average of passed quantum experiment validators Equal sub-weights 0.20 for each: COW, Atom Interferometry, Decoherence, Quantum Clock, Lagrangian Grounding
Predictions 0.30 1 if beats SOTA, else 0.5 + 0.5*exp(improvement/100) Sub-weights: CMB 0.25, PTA GW 0.25, Primordial GWs 0.20, Future Detectors 0.15, Novel Signatures 0.15
Trajectory 0.05 Weighted sum of exp(-best_loss/scale) per particle type vs baselines Particles (each 0.20): electron, photon, proton, neutrino, graviton. Loss types: trajectory_mse, ricci, etc.
Unification 0.20 Sum of sub-scores for unification aspects Sub-weights: SM bridging 0.30 (1 or 0.5), Novel predictions 0.30 (1 if present), Scale hierarchy 0.20 (1 or 0.5), Field content 0.20 (1 if complete)

Bonuses

Bonus Multiplier Condition

All Tests Passed 1.1 100% pass rate in constraints and observational

Penalties

Final Calculation

final_score = min(1.0, base_score * bonus_multiplier * penalty_multiplier * category_multiplier)
Additional 1.1 multiplier if all constraints and observational tests passed.
